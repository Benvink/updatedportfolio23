C-Suite Data Visualisation project

From the initial ideation to final launch, I led a project to implement a business wide data dashboard that enabled business decisions and succinct objectives and key results.

What was the problem?

The team never had a clear overview of business metrics that mattered the most. All decisions were based on data that was either old or irrelevant. Furthermore, the data had been manipulated by multiple parties making it inaccurate and unreliable.

  • Lacked of informed decision making
  • Inability to track performance
  • Inefficient allocation of capital and human resource

What was the minimum viable product?

I designed visual wireframe using Miro to showcase a potential dashboard. Once approved to work on it as a project, I recruited the engineers to build a script that would pull information from AWS on a scheduled basis to a Google spreadsheet. From there, I used Googleâ€™s Looker studio to visualise the information.

What did the initial product look like?

With the initial MVP complete, I was able to bring more comprehensive information into the dashboard, merge data and visualise customer journeys, the overall health of the platform, new sign-ups, repeat buyers, how many times they were purchasing, etc.

The final product

An all-in-one dashboard that allowed managers and teams to see how they were performing, along with goal attainment along with visualised information.

After taking the teams through the various stages of the product life cycle, we were able to bring clarity and objectivity to the decision-making process.

B2B Product

I was part of the team that led to the implementation of the business product. From the initial identification of the problem through to the growth of the product, I was involved in each stage of the process.

What was the problem?

Through interviews and inbound conversations with the customer success and sales teams, it became clear that there was a need for specific type of "platform" or user journey designed for business. The challenge was to build a whole host of tax compliancys, legal entity onboarding flows and "whitelabled" dashboards that reflected the clients objectives.

  • Complicated KYB process
  • Onboarding was optimised for individuals and not businesses.

What was the minimum viable product?

A simple landing page was created to understand the initial need for a business solution. The landing page was incorporated into sales emails. Traffic and sign-ups were monitored over a 2-week period. During that time, we assessed who was signing up and why. After receiving a flurry of business requests, we decided to update the sign-up form to include a drop-down menu for business.

What did the initial product look like?

Revamping the entire onboarding process had to include tax records and business information (legal entity name, legal structure, licences, etc). We first incorporated legal names and key personal information into the sign-up form once a user had initially entered the name of the company and their email address.

The final product

The final product consisted of new entities within the database to determine who was a legal entity vs an individual. Individuals could purchase in their own private capacity as well as purchasing on behalf of a company. Having the separate entities allowed the development team to create a streamlined personalised registration experience. Furthermore, customised white labelled dashboards allowed employees of the company to access their own business dashboards and see how the company as a whole was collectively making an impact.

Outcome

With the initial launch of the revamped process, I was able to measure how many businesses vs individuals were signing up.

  • 30% increase business entity sign-ups over the first quarter from the initial launch
  • Projects sold out 3x times faster as a result of having businesses purchasing.
  • 'Taxation' and 'How do I sign up as a business' queries dropped to 0.
